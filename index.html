On Weighted Principal Hessian Direction (WPHD) Tests Marginal Predictor Contribution for Response in the Metric Space

July 2020 ‚Äì May 2022

Adviser: Prof. Zhou Yu

School of Statistics, East China Normal University

  • Proposed a new marginal coordinate test based on WPHD to evaluate the marginal contribution of response variables to a large number of covariates in the metric space, which filled the gap in the analysis of the data situated in a generic metric space (non-Euclidean data)
  • Designed the permutation test to derive the null distribution of marginal coordinate test, which addressed the issue of predictor selection for regression models with non-linear responses
  • Established and proved the theoretical properties of large samples under the null hypothesis, which guaranteed the validity of the permutation test procedure with WPHD
  • Simulated responses in different metric spaces for the active predictors and the inactive predictors, which verified the performance of our proposals and showed that it is computationally intensive when the sample size is large
  • Demonstrated the utility of this method in analysis of the stroke data
